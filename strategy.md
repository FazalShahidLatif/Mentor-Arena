## PART 1 — CORE CONCEPT (the spine of everything)

### The One-Sentence Positioning
> "Build one real, working project in 150 live hours — with 1-to-1 mentorship, in a small batch, in Pakistan, with a mentor who has actually shipped code."

### The 5 Non-Negotiable Principles
Every copywriting task, advertisement decision, and overall project direction filters through these guidelines:

1. **Live project, not slides.**  
   Every student graduates with one deployed project they can show to employers/clients. This is the headline promise. No course without a shipped project.
2. **1-to-1 + small batch, never big batch.**  
   Maximum 6 students per cohort. Mentor reviews every student's code/PR/design weekly. This is the differentiator vs. Coursera, Udemy, YouTube, or legacy multimedia academies.
3. **Pakistan-first, geo-explicit.**  
   Pricing in PKR, examples tailored for the Pakistani market, payment via JazzCash/EasyPaisa/bank transfer, WhatsApp contact, hours aligned perfectly to PKT. Never say "international" until the 100-review milestone.
4. **Real mentor, real credentials.**  
   Fazal Shahid Latif's bio, his real project history, and his real teaching experience. No generic "team of experts."
5. **Outcome-anchored, not feature-anchored.**  
   Lead with what the student will actually possess (deployed project, GitHub portfolio, freelance-ready skill) — not simply what they'll learn (modules, lessons).

### Three courses, three real projects
| Course | The shipped-project outcome |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Full-Stack Web Development** | A working MERN-stack web app (your choice of domain) deployed on Vercel/Railway, with custom domain, GitHub repo, and clean README. |

## PART 5 — GOOGLE REVIEWS STRATEGY (the 100-review path)

### 5.1 The 100-Review Target
* **Averages:** ~5-10 reviews/week is the ideal natural development curve.
* 100 reviews in 6 months is the conservative baseline; 4 months is the aggressive (but safe) target.

### 5.2 Review Gating & Ethical Policy
* **✅ DO:**
  * Send a direct, non-incentivized request to every past student 7–14 days after graduation.
  * Provide a direct link to the GBP review form.
  * Respond to every single review (positive or negative) within 48 hours.
  * Print a feedback QR code on completion certificates.
* **❌ DON'T:**
  * Incentivize reviews through certificates, products, or discounts.
  * Filter/gate customers based on satisfaction prior to prompting.
  * Post false/simulated testimonials using employee or family accounts.
  * Deploy automated iPad review terminals inside classrooms.

### 5.3 Review Generation Cadence
* 1 cohort (6 students) ➡️ Expect ~3-4 reviews.
* To reach 100 reviews in 6 months, scale up to concurrently running cohorts under strict small-batch controls.

### 5.4 Website Integration
* Custom layout element pulling authentic Google review feeds.
* Use clean Schema markup for ratings.
* Proved aggregate badge: `"★★★★★ 4.9/5 from Google Reviews"`. Avoid showing stars if review count is critically low. Show organic raw feedback.
